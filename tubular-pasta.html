Tubular Pasta

  • Bucatini is a long, straw-like pasta that is hollow in the middle. The name derives from the Italian word "buco", which means "hole". The hollow nature of bucatini ensure optimal sauce coverage throughout the noodle!

  • Cavatappi is a macaroni-style swirly noodle that pairs well with vegetable-based suaces and lean meats. In Italian, Cavatappi means "corkscrew".

  • My personal favorite pasta shape, ditalini is a short, bead-like noodle that reminds me of my elementary school days (did anybody else's cafeteria serve ditalini?). My favorite way to use ditalini is in a tomato vodka sauce with vegtables.

  • Paccheri is a large, tubular pasta originating from Campania. Pair paccheri with any dish that features vegetables small enough to "hide" in the paccheri noodles. Each bite will be a delicious surprise!

  • A popular pasta shape, penne is a ribbed and angled short pasta shape. In Italian, penne means "pen", referencing the similarities of the noodle's angled edges to a quill pen.

  • Tortiglioni is a short, tubular pasta with spiralized ribbing across it's surface. Deriving from the Latin word torquere, which means "to twist", the ridges on tortiglioni noodles help retain sauce.
